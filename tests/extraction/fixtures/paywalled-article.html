The Future of Quantum Computing: Beyond the Hype

By Michael Harris in San Francisco · December 20, 2024

Quantum computing has been the "next big thing" for over a decade. But 2024 marks a turning point. Three major milestones suggest the technology is finally moving from laboratory curiosities toward practical applications. IBM's 1,121-qubit processor, Google's demonstration of quantum error correction below the surface code threshold, and the first hybrid classical-quantum workloads running in production environments all point in the same direction.

However, significant challenges remain. Error rates on current quantum processors are still orders of magnitude too high for most practical applications. The qubit coherence times, while improving, limit the depth of circuits that can be executed reliably. And the software ecosystem remains immature, with few developers trained in quantum algorithm design. Industry analysts project that fault-tolerant quantum computing capable of breaking current encryption standards remains at least a decade away.

Investment Landscape

Despite these challenges, venture capital investment in quantum computing reached $3.2 billion in 2024, up from $1.8 billion in 2023. Governments have committed an additional $8.5 billion in research funding across the US, EU, and China. The question is no longer whether quantum computing will matter, but when and for which applications first.
