Data Privacy and Security Regulations 2024

Federal Trade Commission · Docket No. FTC-2024-0031 · Published December 1, 2024

Section 1 — Scope and Applicability

These regulations shall apply to any entity that collects, processes, or stores the personal data of more than 100,000 individuals within the United States, or whose annual revenue derived from the sale of personal data exceeds 25 million dollars. Entities with fewer than 500 employees may qualify for reduced compliance requirements under Section 4(c).

Section 2 — Data Collection Requirements

Covered entities must obtain explicit opt-in consent from individuals before collecting any sensitive personal data, including but not limited to biometric data, health information, financial account numbers, and precise geolocation data. Consent must be obtained through a clear and conspicuous mechanism separate from general terms of service. The request for consent shall include: (a) the specific categories of data to be collected, (b) the purpose for collection, (c) the duration of data retention, and (d) the entities with whom the data may be shared.

Section 3 — Security Standards

Covered entities shall implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal data. At minimum, these safeguards must include encryption of data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent, encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.3 or higher, and annual third-party security audits. Breach notification must be provided to affected individuals within 72 hours of discovery.

Section 4 — Enforcement

Violations of these regulations may result in civil penalties of up to 50,000 dollars per violation, or 4% of annual global revenue, whichever is greater. The Commission may also require corrective action plans, independent audits for a period of up to 5 years, and restitution to affected individuals.
